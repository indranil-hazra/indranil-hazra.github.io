    Principle Investigator

    Dr. Indranil Hazra

    Assistant Professor, IIT Kharagpur

    I am an Assistant Professor of Reliability Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur (IITKGP), Kharagpur, India. I am currently working broadly in the domain of structural reliability and health monitoring. In particular, I am interested in exploring the following areas: probabilistic finite element analysis, structural digital twin technology, dynamic fault tree analysis, stochastic deterioration modeling, approximate Bayesian computation, and quantum machine learning.

    Before joining IITKGP, I was an Assistant Professor at the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur (IITJ), Karwar, India. I was a Postdoctoral Associate at the University of Maryland (UMD), College Park, USA, where I developed a computational framework for offline/online reliability-based performance optimization of unmanned surface vessels. Prior to that, I held a Postdoctoral Fellowship at the University of Waterloo (UW), Waterloo, Canada, where I developed a spatial statistical modeling methodology for uncertainty modeling of radioactive contaminant inventories.

    I pursued my PhD at the University of Waterloo (UW), Waterloo, Canada, focusing on likelihood-free Bayesian inference of stochastic degradation models. During my PhD at UW, I received several awards, including the International Doctoral Student Award, University of Waterloo Senate Graduate Scholarship, and the Donald E. Grierson Memorial Award. I completed MEng at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, India, and BEng at Jadavpur University (JU), Kolkata, India. Prior to joining PhD, I was a Project Associate at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IITM), Chennai, India.

    Research

    Probabilistic Finite Element Analysis

    We leverage probabilistic finite element methods for reliability assessment of engineering structures. Utilizing advanced probabilistic tools integrated with finite element methods, we evaluate and enhance the reliability and quality of structural designs. Our approach includes conducting uncertainty analysis to quantify the effects of variable inputs on structural behavior, performing reliability analysis to calculate failure probabilities, and executing reliability-based optimization. Additionally, we focus on robust design to minimize sensitivity to uncertainties, ensuring predictable and dependable performance of various structures.

    Structural Digital Twin Technology

    We focus on developing streamlined interfaces to integrate digital twin technology into structural applications, enabling efficient real-time health monitoring and predictive maintenance. By creating a digital replica of physical structures, we enhance system performance through continuous data collection and analysis. This allows for proactive identification of potential issues, reducing downtime and maintenance costs. Our approach ensures that structural integrity is maintained while optimizing operational efficiency, providing a robust solution for modern infrastructure management.

    Dynamic Fault Tree Analysis

    We employ dynamic fault tree analysis to assess complex system reliability, integrating time-dependent factors and evolving states. Through advanced modeling and probabilistic computations, we aim to deliver accurate risk assessments for critical infrastructure and unmanned systems. Our work contributes to safer operations by accurately predicting and mitigating potential failures in dynamic and variable engineering environments, ensuring reliable performance and system safety.

    Stochastic Deterioration Modeling

    We specialize in stochastic deterioration modeling to predict and manage the aging of engineering structures. By incorporating randomness and uncertainty into our models, we accurately simulate the progressive degradation of materials over time. This approach enables us to assess the remaining service life and reliability of structures, informing maintenance and repair strategies. Our advanced modeling techniques ensure optimized resource allocation and enhanced safety, providing a comprehensive solution for managing structural integrity under uncertain conditions.

    Approximate Bayesian Computation

    We develop advanced likelihood-free algorithms for efficient Bayesian inference, specializing in model selection and parameter estimation from noisy and limited condition monitoring data. Our expertise encompasses Hamiltonian Monte Carlo-based, subset simulation-based likelihood-free algorithms, and more. These methods extend beyond condition monitoring to applications such as structural system identification, degradation modeling, life prediction, and Bayesian reliability analysis. Through innovative approaches, we aim to enhance computational efficiency and accuracy, facilitating robust analysis in various domains.

    Quantum Machine Learning

    We explore quantum computing and machine learning to devise tailored solutions for challenges in reliability simulation, health monitoring, remaining useful life estimation, surrogate modeling, and reliability analysis of complex engineering systems. Our research integrates cutting-edge quantum computing techniques with machine learning to pioneer hybrid approaches in addressing these multifaceted issues. By synergizing quantum computing's potential with machine learning's adaptability, we strive to pave the way for significant advancements in reliability assessment and predictive modeling across diverse domains.
